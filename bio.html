Biography

Nicolas Cage (born Nicolas Kim Coppola) is an American actor and filmmaker known for his intense and often eccentric performances across a wide range of films. He began acting in the early 1980s and has since appeared in dramas, comedies, action films, and thrillers.

Early life

Born on January 7, 1964, in Long Beach, California, Cage comes from a family with strong ties to the film industry. He adopted the stage name "Cage" to forge his own path as an actor.

Career highlights

  • Breakout roles in the 1980s and early 1990s.
  • Academy Award for Best Actor for Leaving Las Vegas (1995).
  • Known for memorable roles in films such as Adaptation, Face/Off, National Treasure, and many cult favorites.
